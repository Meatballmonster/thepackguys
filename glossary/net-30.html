Net-30.

Trade credit payment terms

Payment terms where the full invoice is due 30 days from the invoice date. Standard B2B credit term in wholesale packaging.

Net-30 means: the seller ships goods, sends an invoice, and the buyer has 30 calendar days from the invoice date to pay the full amount. No interest accrues during the 30 days. After day 30, late fees or interest can apply per contract.

Net-30 is working capital. The buyer holds onto cash for 30 days before paying — that's effectively a 30-day, zero-interest loan from the supplier. For cannabis brands scaling fast, this is meaningful: a Net-30 line lets the buyer sell-through the product before paying for it.

Pack Guys extends Net-30 to wholesale accounts after their second paid invoice clears. No separate credit application, no trade references demanded, no buyer-portal demo call. Read the working-capital math for when Net-30 beats prepay.
