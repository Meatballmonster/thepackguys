ASTM D3475.

Standard Classification of Child-Resistant Packages

The American Society for Testing and Materials’ standard classification system for child-resistant packaging — defines the test protocols used to verify CR closures.

ASTM D3475 is a classification system, not a single test. It defines categories of child-resistant closures (squeeze-and-turn, push-down-and-turn, squeeze pop-top, etc.) and references the underlying test protocols — usually 16 CFR § 1700.20 — that each design must pass.

When a packaging supplier says a tube is "ASTM D3475 certified," they mean the closure has been classified under ASTM's framework and tested per the federal protocol. The two terms (ASTM D3475 and 16 CFR § 1700.20) appear together because one is the classification, the other is the actual test law.

Pack Guys 116mm squeeze pop-top tubes are certified to ASTM D3475 and tested per 16 CFR § 1700.20. The lab test PDF is available to verified wholesale accounts within 24 hours of request.

related terms

  • 16 CFR § 1700.20 — the actual federal test law ASTM D3475 references
  • CR certification — the umbrella term for child-resistance compliance
  • Pop-top — one of the closure types ASTM D3475 classifies
