Pop-top.

Pre-roll tube closure type

A child-resistant closure that opens by squeezing the cap (not twisting) — designed to be defeated by adults but not by children.

A pop-top is a class of child-resistant closure where the cap is removed by squeezing the sides of the cap (typically at two opposite points) to release a snap mechanism, then pulling up. It's distinct from twist-off (push-down-and-turn) closures.

Two pop-top sub-types: squeeze pop-top (cap snaps off in one motion) and hinged pop-top (cap stays attached, swings open). Pack Guys 116mm tubes use the squeeze pop-top variant — the cap fully detaches, no hinge to break over time.

Pop-top closures pass 16 CFR § 1700.20 because the squeeze motion requires adult-level fine motor coordination that most children under 4 cannot reliably execute. The magenta squeeze chevrons on the Pack Guys tube cap mark where to press.
