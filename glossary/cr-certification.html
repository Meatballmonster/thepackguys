CR Certification.

Child-resistant packaging certification

The verified compliance status that a closure design has passed the federal child-resistance test (16 CFR § 1700.20). Required for cannabis packaging in every adult-use state.

"CR certification" (short for child-resistant) is the verified status that a packaging design has passed a lab test for child-resistance, per the federal protocol 16 CFR § 1700.20. It's legally required for cannabis pre-roll tubes in every adult-use US state.

What CR certification is NOT: a marketing claim. Saying "CR certified" without a lab report is industry slang at best, regulatory non-compliance at worst. The actual artifact of CR certification is a lab-issued PDF naming: the closure design, the test protocol used, the lab that ran it, the date, and the batch tested.

For pre-roll tubes, CR certification is design-level — once a closure design passes, all tubes manufactured to that design carry the cert. Pack Guys 116mm squeeze pop-top closure is CR-certified and the test PDF is available to verified wholesale accounts within 24 hours.

Watch for red flags: vague certification claims, "we'll request the PDF from the factory," missing test dates, generic certificates without batch traceability. A real supplier hands the PDF over in 24 hours.
