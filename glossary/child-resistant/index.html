Resources · Glossary

Child-Resistant.

Property of a closure or package that has been verified to resist opening by children under 5 while still being adult-accessible — per 16 CFR § 1700.20.

"Child-resistant" is a precise regulatory term, not a marketing description. A package is child-resistant only if its closure design has passed the test defined in 16 CFR § 1700.20: 80%+ of children ages 42–51 months cannot open it in 10 minutes, AND 90%+ of adults CAN open it within a reasonable time.

For cannabis pre-roll tubes, child-resistant packaging is mandatory in every US adult-use state. The package must remain CR for the lifetime of the product, not just first-opening (re-sealable CR requirement).

What "child-resistant" isn't: child-PROOF. The standard explicitly allows that some children may eventually open it — the bar is "not within 10 minutes, not without adult-level coordination."
